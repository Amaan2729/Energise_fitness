Bench Press Tutorial

How to Do a Proper Bench Press

  1. Set up: Lie back on a flat bench with your feet firmly planted on the floor. Position your eyes directly under the bar.
  2. Grip the bar: Grasp the bar with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, ensuring a secure grip.
  3. Lift the bar: Unrack the bar and hold it above your chest with arms fully extended.
  4. Lower the bar: Slowly lower the bar to your mid-chest while keeping your elbows at about a 45-degree angle to your body.
  5. Press the bar up: Push the bar back up to the starting position by extending your arms, keeping your feet and back stable on the bench.
  6. Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions while maintaining control and proper form.

Benefits of Bench Press

  • Builds strength in the chest, shoulders, and triceps
  • Improves upper body power
  • Enhances muscle endurance
  • Supports upper body stability and posture
  • Can be modified for different fitness levels and goals

Tips for Perfecting Your Bench Press

  • Keep your feet planted and your back flat on the bench.
  • Engage your core to stabilize your body.
  • Lower the bar in a controlled manner and avoid bouncing it off your chest.
  • Use a spotter for safety when lifting heavy weights.
  • Start with lighter weights to perfect your form before progressing to heavier loads.
