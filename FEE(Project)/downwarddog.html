Downward Dog Tutorial

How to Perform Downward Dog

  1. Start on all fours: Begin with your hands directly under your shoulders and knees under your hips.
  2. Lift your hips: Press into your hands and lift your hips toward the ceiling, forming an inverted "V" shape with your body.
  3. Engage your core: Keep your core tight and lengthen your spine as you push your hips back.
  4. Relax your neck: Let your head hang between your arms with your ears aligned with your upper arms.
  5. Stretch your legs: Try to straighten your legs, pressing your heels toward the ground (even if they don't touch).
  6. Breathe deeply: Hold the position for several breaths, focusing on your breathing and the stretch in your body.

Benefits of Downward Dog

  • Stretches the hamstrings, calves, and shoulders
  • Strengthens the arms, legs, and core
  • Improves posture and alignment
  • Increases flexibility and balance
  • Relieves tension and stress

Tips for Perfecting Your Downward Dog

  • Engage your core to keep your spine long and prevent collapsing your lower back.
  • Focus on pushing your hips up and back rather than pressing your heels to the floor.
  • Distribute weight evenly between your hands and feet to maintain balance.
  • Bend your knees slightly if needed to achieve better alignment.
  • Incorporate this pose into your yoga flow for a full-body stretch and strength.
