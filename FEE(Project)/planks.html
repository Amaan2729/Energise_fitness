Plank Tutorial

How to Do a Proper Plank

  1. Start in a forearm plank position: Place your forearms on the ground, elbows directly under your shoulders. Extend your legs behind you, balancing on your toes.
  2. Engage your core: Tighten your core and glutes, keeping your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  3. Hold the position: Maintain the plank for as long as you can, ensuring your body stays straight and doesnâ€™t sag or lift.
  4. Focus on breathing: Keep your breathing steady while holding the plank to stay relaxed and maintain form.
  5. Rest and repeat: Take breaks between sets and gradually increase the duration as you build strength.

Benefits of Plank

  • Strengthens the core, back, and shoulders
  • Improves posture and stability
  • Engages multiple muscle groups simultaneously
  • Can be done anywhere without equipment
  • Helps in enhancing overall body strength and endurance

Tips for Perfecting Your Plank

  • Ensure your body forms a straight line from head to heels.
  • Avoid letting your hips drop or raise too high.
  • Engage your core and glutes throughout the exercise.
  • Start with shorter holds and gradually increase your plank duration.
  • If you find the standard plank challenging, begin with knee planks.
