Deadlift Tutorial

How to Do a Proper Deadlift

  1. Start standing: Stand with your feet hip-width apart and the barbell over your mid-foot. Grip the barbell with your hands just outside your knees.
  2. Set up: Bend at your hips and knees to lower yourself to the bar. Keep your back straight and chest up.
  3. Grip the bar: Grasp the bar with an overhand grip or a mixed grip (one hand over, one under).
  4. Lift the barbell: With a straight back and engaged core, push through your heels to lift the barbell by extending your hips and knees. Keep the bar close to your body.
  5. Stand tall: At the top of the movement, stand tall with your shoulders back and chest up.
  6. Lower the barbell: Reverse the movement by pushing your hips back and bending your knees. Lower the barbell to the floor with control.

Benefits of Deadlifts

  • Strengthens the entire posterior chain
  • Improves overall body power and strength
  • Enhances core stability and balance
  • Boosts metabolic rate and fat burning
  • Supports functional strength for daily activities

Tips for Perfecting Your Deadlift

  • Keep your back flat and chest up throughout the lift.
  • Avoid rounding your lower back to prevent injury.
  • Engage your core and drive through your heels.
  • Keep the bar close to your body throughout the movement.
  • Practice proper form with lighter weights before progressing to heavier loads.
