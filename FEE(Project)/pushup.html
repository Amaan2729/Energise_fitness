Push-Up Tutorial

How to Do a Proper Push-Up

  1. Start in a plank position: Place your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart on the floor, with your shoulders directly above your wrists. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
  2. Lower your body: Bend your elbows and slowly lower your chest towards the floor, keeping your body straight and your core engaged. Go as low as you can without touching the ground.
  3. Push back up: Press through your palms to straighten your arms and return to the starting position. Keep your movements controlled and avoid locking your elbows at the top.
  4. Repeat: Perform the desired number of repetitions, maintaining good form throughout.

Benefits of Push-Ups

  • Strengthens the chest, shoulders, and triceps
  • Engages the core muscles for stability
  • Improves upper body endurance
  • Can be done anywhere without equipment
  • Scalable to different fitness levels

Tips for Perfecting Your Push-Up

  • Keep your body in a straight line throughout the movement.
  • Avoid letting your hips sag or pike.
  • Engage your core to protect your lower back.
  • If you're new to push-ups, start with modified push-ups on your knees or against a wall.
  • Gradually increase the number of repetitions as you build strength.
