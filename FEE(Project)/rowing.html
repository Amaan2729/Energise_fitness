Rowing Tutorial

How to Do a Proper Rowing Exercise

  1. Start with the Catch Position: Sit on the rower with your knees bent, shins vertical, and hands gripping the handle. Lean forward slightly with your arms extended.
  2. Drive: Push through your legs while straightening them, then lean back and pull the handle towards your chest. Engage your core and maintain a straight posture.
  3. Finish: Your legs should be extended, your torso leaning back slightly, and the handle should be at your mid-chest level.
  4. Recovery: Reverse the movement by extending your arms, leaning forward, and bending your knees to return to the starting position.
  5. Repeat: Maintain a steady rhythm for the desired duration, focusing on form and breathing.

Tips for Effective Rowing

  • Maintain a straight back and engage your core throughout the movement.
  • Focus on using your legs first, then your back and arms.
  • Avoid hunching your shoulders; keep them relaxed.
  • Start with shorter sessions and gradually increase duration as you build endurance.
  • Practice maintaining a steady rhythm for a smooth workout.
